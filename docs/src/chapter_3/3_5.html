### 3.5: Pressure * To make sense of some effects in which a force acts on an object we have to consider not only the force but also the area on which it acts. * For example: * Wearing skis prevents you sinking into soft snow because your weight is spread over a greater area. * A nail is designed with a small area of contact which allows the nail to be hammered into the wood easier.
### 3.5: Pressure > $$ \text{pressure} = \displaystyle \frac{\text{force}}{\text{area}} $$ > or > $$ p = \displaystyle \frac{F}{A}$$ The SI Unit of pressure is called **Pascal** ($Pa$). However, because one $Pa$ is pretty small, the kiloPascal ($kPa$) and megaPascal ($mPa$) are more commonly used.
