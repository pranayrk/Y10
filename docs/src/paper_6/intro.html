#### About IGCSE Physics 0625 * You will be entered for three examination papers, *two theory papers* and *one practical paper*. * These are **Paper 2**, **Paper 4**, **Paper 6** * **Paper 2:** 40 multiple choice questions. * **Paper 4:** More descriptive questions * **Paper 6:** Alternative to Practical
### What will be on Paper 6? You may be asked questions on the following experimental contexts: * measurement of physical quantities such as length or volume or force * springs and balances * timing motion or oscillations * optics equipment such as mirrors, prisms and lenses * cooling and heating * electric circuits * procedures using simple apparatus, in situations where the method may not be familiar to you.
### Tips for Paper 6 * When plotting a graph * choose the scales so that the plots occupy more than half of the graph grid. * You should always use a sharp pencil * plot small, neat, accurately placed crosses. * Then draw a neat thin best-fit line. * You should understand that if $y$ is proportional to $x$ then the graph will be a straight line through the origin.
### Tips for Paper 6 * Diagrams should be drawn with care using a sharp pencil. * Column headings in tables of readings must be headed with the quantity and unit * Graph axes are labelled in the same way. * Final answers should be given to *two or three significant figures*.
### Tips for Paper 6 * When carrying out practical work there are usually measurements that are in some way difficult to take in spite of taking great care. * You should comment about these difficulties when asked about precautions taken to improve accuracy. * If you are asked to justify a statement that you have made it must be justified by reference to the readings. A theoretical justification in a practical test will not gain marks. * **Identify the sensitivity of the apparatus** and record the readings per the sensitivity of the apparatus
### Tips for Paper 6 * Include a good diagram, key factor of investigation, proper steps, independent dependant and control variables along with a solid conclusion * Its always good to add a formula in the conclusion. * Go though past papers and see the expected answers. Usually they tend to overlap a lot
