• Energy is defined as the ability to do work, which means having the capacity to cause some change in this world.
  • The SI unit of energy is the Joule
  • Energy can be observed in the form of energy stores and energy transfers

Energy Stores: energy that is waiting to do work.

Energy Transfers: is energy that is in the process of moving from one energy store to another.

There is normally some work done in the process

Some ways energy can be transferred are by forces, electrical currents, heating, and by electromagnetic, sound and other waves

Energy transferred results in that amount of work

Work means some change or effect in this world.

The SI unit of work is Joule which is the same unit as energy

For work done when a force moves an object, we can use the equation:

$$ \text{work done} = \text{force applied} \times \text{distance moved in the direction of force} $$

$$ W = F d$$

If all forces are in balance, there is no motion.

There is no work done when there is no motion.

In some cases, there is motion, but still no work done.

A satellite orbiting the Earth does no work

This is because at every point, the satellite moves in a direction perpendicular to the direction of gravitational force

So there is no movement in the direction of force applied

So $$W = Fd = F \times 0 = 0 \; J$$

Kinetic energy is an energy transfer that occurs when an object is in motion.

Kinetic energy of an object can be calculated by

$$ \text{Kinetic energy} = \frac{1}{2} \times \text{mass} \times \text{velocity}^2$$

$$ E_k = \frac{1}{2} m v^2 $$

When an object is raised, it gains an amount of gravitational potential energy

Gravitational potential energy is an energy store which is ready to do the work of pulling the object closer to the Earth

The gravitational potential energy gained or lost by the object can be calculated by

$$ \text{gravitational p.e} = \text{mass} \times \text{acc. due to gravity} \times \text{change in height} $$

$$ E_p = m g \Delta h$$

When gravitational potential energy is allowed to do work, it causes the object to fall down towards the Earth

This causes an increase in the objects kinetic energy in the downward direction

The gravitational potential energy gained and lost does not depend on the path taken.

It only depends on the difference between initial and final heights

In a pendulum, the energy transfers continuously between gravitational potential energy and kinetic energy

The gravitational potential energy is maximum at the ends of the arc

The kinetic energy is maximum at the lowest point of the arc

How fast an object does work is measured in its power

Power is defined as the work done per unit time

Power can also be defined as the energy transferred per unit time

$$ \text{power} = \frac{\text{Work}}{\text{time}} = \frac{\text{Energy transferred}}{\text{time}}$$

$$ P = \frac{W}{t} = \frac{\Delta E}{t}$$

The SI unit of power is the Watt which is 1 joule per second

Efficiency is the ratio of useful energy output to the total energy input in a system or process.

$$ \text{efficiency} = \frac{\text{useful energy output}}{\text{total energy input}} $$

It can also be calculated based on the power

$$ \text{efficiency} = \frac{\text{useful power output}}{\text{total power input}} $$

There is no unit for efficiency, since it is just a number
