### 4.4: Efficiency and Power * The **power** of a thing is the work it does per second, i.e. the rate at which it does work. * This is the same as the rate at which it transfers energy from one store to another. * The SI unit of power is the **Watt** ($W$) and is a rate of working of $1J$ per second > $$\text{power} = \frac{\text{work done}}{\text{time taken}} = \frac{\text{energy transferred}}{\text{time taken}}$$

For power involved in motion:

### 4.4: Efficiency and Power $$\begin{aligned} \text{total energy input} & = \text{useful energy output} \\\\ & \quad + \text{wasted energy output} \end{aligned}$$ * The **efficiency** of a device is the percentage of the energy supplied to it that is useful > $$\text{efficiency} = \frac{\text{useful work done}}{\text{total energy input}} \times 100\\%$$
### 4.4: Efficiency and Power * A device is **efficient** if it transfers energy mainly to useful stores and the lost energy is small. * Another way that efficiency can be calculated is to use *power*: > $$\text{efficiency} = \frac{\text{useful power output}}{\text{total power input}} \times 100 \\%$$
