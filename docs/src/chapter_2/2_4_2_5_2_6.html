### 2.4 : Free Fall * **Acceleration of free fall** is the *constant* downward acceleration applied on an object which makes it fall down * This acceleration is represented by the symbol $g$. * On Earth, the acceleration measures to be $9.8m/s^2$. * On the moon, it is $1.62m/s^2$ * On Jupiter, it is $24.9m/s^2$

### 2.4 : Free Fall * Without air resistance, gravity causes an acceleration of $9.8m/s^2$. * With air resistance: * The object falls and its speed increases * The faster an object moves, the more air resistance it faces * Eventually air resistance and gravity are equal and opposite * No resultant acceleration, so the speed becomes constant * This constant speed is known as the **terminal velocity**
### 2.4 : Free Fall The terminal velocity of a human is around $200km/h$. The terminal velocity of an ant is around $6km/h$. The terminal velocity of an object depends on the weight, size and shape: * A small dense object has a high terminal velocity * A light object with larger area has a low terminal velocity
