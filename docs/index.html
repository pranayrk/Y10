Since its is inconvenient to model every atom in a gas, we normally consider the average effect of all these collisions

The force from one collision is small but as there are many particles colliding all of the time, the force exerted is large.

### 3.8, 5.4: Gas Laws $\begin{aligned} P \propto \frac{1}{V} & \implies P = \text{constant} \times \frac{1}{V} \\ & \implies P \times V = \text{constant} \end{aligned}$ > This equation is **Boyle's Law** > $$P \times V = \text{constant}$$ > or > $$ P_1 \times V_1 = P_2 \times V_2$$
