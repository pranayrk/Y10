The variation of the pressure of a fixed mass of gas with changes in volume (at constant temperature) is shown

Examination of the curve shows that if $p$ is doubled, $V$ is halved.

The graph is an isothermal graph, which means that the temperature at all points in the graph is kept the same

* We can observe $p$ is directly proportional to $\frac{1}{V}$ (since the graph is a straight line) * So, $p$ is inversely proportional to $V$. $$p \propto \frac{1}{V}$$ * Changing the proportionality to an equality: $$ p = \text{constant} \times \frac{1}{V}$$
> $$ p \times V = \text{constant}$$ * If several pairs of readings, $p_1$ and $V_1$, $p_2$ and $V_2$, etc. are taken, then it can be confirmed that $$ p_1 V_1 = p_2 V_2 = \text{constant}$$ > This is known as **Boyle's Law** which is stated as > **The pressure of a fixed mass of gas is inversely proportional to its volume if its temperature is kept constant**
