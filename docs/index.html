### Spring Experiment A student is investigating how the material of a spring affects its behaviour when stretched. The following apparatus is available to the student: * wires of different thickness, length and material * a set of 10 g masses and a set of 100 g masses, both with hangers * a wooden rod approximately 1 cm in diameter * other standard laboratory equipment. Plan an experiment which will enable you to test the extension of springs made from different types of wire.

Spring Experiment

In your plan, you should include

1. instructions for making a spring from the wire that is provided

springs made by winding wire around rod (or similar)

2. what you will measure

take different readings under different loads for each spring material

Spring Experiment

3. instructions for carrying out the experiment

  • apply load, measure length / extension of spring
  • repeat for spring(s) of different material
  • record results in suitable annotated table / bar chart / graph

4. the variables you will keep the same to ensure the comparison is a fair test

  • unstretched length of spring
  • diameter of wire
  • coil spacing
  • load / range of loads used
  • diameter of spring

Spring Experiment

5. any precaution which should be taken or difficulty which might occur

  • clamp the retort stand or it might topple
  • use small loads or spring might overstretch
  • loads need to apply force smoothly / slowly
  • have a workable arrangement for applying load to spring (e.g. small loop at end of spring)

6. Describe how you will conduct the experiment and ensure accuracy of results

  • trial experiment to find (range of) loads to use
  • how to determine extension of spring
  • repeat each reading and take average
  • at least 5 different loads for each sample if producing graph
